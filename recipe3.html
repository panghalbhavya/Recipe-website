CHOWMEIN

Homemade chow mein is a 30-minute stir-fry dish featuring thin, chewy noodles, crisp vegetables (cabbage, carrots, celery, bean sprouts), and a savory soy-oyster sauce. Key steps involve boiling noodles until just tender, rinsing them cold, and stir-frying with soy sauce, sugar, and toasted sesame oil for a caramelized, authentic flavor.

Key Components & Ingredients:

  • Noodles: Thin Cantonese egg noodles, yakisoba, or ramen noodles.
  • Vegetables: Shredded cabbage, celery, carrots, green onions, and bean sprouts.
  • Sauce: Mix cup soy sauce, 2 tbsp oyster sauce, 1 tbsp sesame oil, 1 tsp cornstarch, and 1 tbsp brown sugar

Steps to Make Chow Mein:


Prepare Noodles: Boil noodles until just cooked (slightly under), rinse with cold water to remove starch, and toss with a little oil to prevent sticking.
Stir-fry Aromatics/Veggies: In a wok or skillet over high heat, stir-fry garlic and ginger, followed by vegetables until tender-crisp.
Combine and Sauce: Add the noodles and sauce to the wok. Toss rapidly for 1–2 minutes until the sauce thickens and coats everything, leaving it to char for 10–15 seconds for smoky flavor.
Finish: Add bean sprouts last and cook for another 30 seconds before serving.
