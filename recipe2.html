BURGER AND FRIES

To make juicy homemade burgers, use 80/20 ground beef, forming four 1-inch thick patties with a dimple in the center to prevent swelling. Season generously with salt and pepper right before grilling or pan-frying on medium-high heat for 3–5 minutes per side. Toast buns with butter, add cheese, and serve

Key Tips for Success:

  • Don't Overwork Meat: Handle the beef gently to keep it tender.
  • Proper Ratios: Use 80% lean, 20% fat ground beef for maximum juiciness.
  • Size Matters: Make patties roughly 1 inch wider than the bun to account for shrinkage.
  • Create a Dimple: Press a small indentation in the center of the patty so it cooks flat, not domed.
  • Sear Well: Cook on a hot surface (for smash burgers) to develop a deep brown crust


Steps to make a burger:

Prepare Patties: Divide 1 lb of beef into 4 equal portions and shape into patties without overworking the meat.
Season: Season generously with salt and pepper on both sides just before cooking
Cook: Grill or pan-fry on medium-high heat for 3–5 minutes per side. For cheeseburgers, add cheese in the last 1–2 minutes.
Rest: Let the patties rest for a few minutes to keep them juicy
Assemble: Toast buns, add condiments (mayonnaise, mustard, ketchup), and top with toppings like lettuce, tomato, onions, and pickles
